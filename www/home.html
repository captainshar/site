What is this Krazy Thang?

tl;dr

Super Easy Kubernetes Cloud and Downloadable Apps on Raspberry Pi's. Install it RIGHT MEOW.

Long, Rambling, Dad Story

Ok, awhile back I took Kubernetes threw it on Raspberry Pi's and created multi-microservice App that yells at my kids to get ready in the morning (in an Australian accent).

No, not kidding. This is literally how my kids get ready in the morning now.

Originally, I did this as a joke because I was bored. I was thinking of controlling my window A/C unit with a Raspberry Pi, having not found a challenging enough project (Retro Pi is so damn easy). But then I thought, that's totally overkill. That's way too much unused processing power. But then I thought, I could use that extra power as a home cloud, having created little Apps for my kids to play with. Going further, I could probably do something with Kubernetes, making it easier to deploy changes vs. SSH'ing or even burning SD cards. So I set out on a crazy, probalby impossible journey to put Kubernetes on Raspberry Pi's

Here's the weird thing: it worked.

Beyond that success, I realized I had created a platform. When I say I installed Kuberenetes and the App runs on that, I'm not kidding. I have Pods interacting with the Pi's GPIO; DaemonSets deploying to Node that I simply label that they have a Button; those DaemonSets pushing events to Redis channels; Daemons reacting to those events, saying Chores are done, and on and on.

What's really cool is the kids like to use this (called Nandy), creating their own chores or just chaning the order, etc. Since my kids and are having a blast with all this, I thought I'd share what I'd done.

So what this does is allow you to easily grab a handful of Raspberry Pi's, install Kuberenetes on them. Hit up Install for how.

Once running, you can install Apps from repos I created in GitHub. Right now, I just have the Redis service, but I'm looking to add more. Check out Apps for more.

If you're curious on how it all work, head over to Architecture.

And if you're eager to write your own Apps, AWESOME. Development is just for you.
